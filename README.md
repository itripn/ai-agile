# ai-agile

A working body of design documents that follow from a claim:
the product and software development lifecycles refined over the
past decade were calibrated for a world that AI-assisted work has
begun to dismantle, and the frameworks teams already run on need a
deliberate redesign rather than abandonment.

The short, public-facing version of the argument is the manifesto at
**[ai-agile.dev](https://ai-agile.dev)**. Sign it if you see what we see.
The documents here are the longer, more honest version — they say what
breaks, why, and what we think replaces it.

## Read in this order

1. **[The position paper](./01-AI-Assisted%20Velocity%20and%20the%20Limits%20of%20Modern%20PDLC-SDLC.md)** —

2. **[Unit-of-Work Design Spec](./02-Unit-of-Work%20Design%20Spec%20-%20The%20Validated%20Change.md)** —
   redefining what "a piece of work" is when production is cheap.
3. **[Production Standards Design Spec](./03-Production%20Standards%20Design%20Spec.md)** —
   what the artifact must meet, regardless of who or what produced it.
4. **[Role and Gating Design Spec](./04-Role%20and%20Gating%20Design%20Spec.md)** —
   who approves, who refines, who rejects, and where the gates live.
5. **[Modified Cadence Design Spec](./05-Modified%20Cadence%20Design%20Spec.md)** —
   what replaces sprint planning, PI planning, and the rituals tied to them.

[**Terminology Cheat Sheet**](./Terminology%20Cheat%20Sheet.md) —
glossary of the terms used across the specs.

## Status

Incomplete on purpose. These documents argue a direction and propose
concrete mechanics, but they are stress-tests, not finished standards.
The intent is for them to be read, argued with, broken, and rebuilt
in public.
